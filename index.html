05

Interests

My scientific interests centre on the development of next-generation X-ray instrumentation — particularly sample delivery systems and detector technologies that unlock new regimes in time-resolved structural biology. I am drawn to problems at the boundary of physics, engineering, and chemistry, especially where bespoke instrumentation enables experiments previously considered impossible.

Beyond the lab, I am fascinated by the history and sociology of science — how scientific knowledge is produced, whose bodies and ecologies it draws on, and how methodological choices carry social consequence. This interest runs through my work on gut microbiome research and my involvement with the Australia and New Zealand History of Medicine Society.
